Grandma's famous muffins

"Life’s a journey," your grandma used to say, "and sometimes you need a structure to cross from memory to meaning." These muffins were her signature, and rumor has it they hold more than just flavor.

I decided to share this recipe with you to keep this wonderful family secret going, I know your mom never was much of a cook and learned very little from her own mother after all.

Ingredients

  • Brown sugar - 1/2 cup
  • All-purpose flour - 1 3/4 cups
  • Raspberries - 1 cup (fresh or frozen)
  • Baking powder - 1 tsp
  • Instant oats - 3/4 cup
  • Vanilla extract - 1 tsp
  • Dark chocolate chips - 1/2 cup
  • Salt - 1/2 tsp
  • Ginger (ground) - 1 tsp
  • Milk - 3/4 cup
  • Eggs - 2
  • Melted butter - 1/2 cup

Instructions

  1. Preheat the oven to 375°F and line a muffin tin.
  2. In a large bowl, combine the flour, oats, baking powder, salt, brown sugar, and ginger.
  3. In another bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk, vanilla extract, and melted butter.
  4. Gently fold the wet ingredients into the dry mixture until just combined.
  5. Stir in the raspberries and dark chocolate chips.
  6. Fill each muffin cup 3/4 full and bake for 18–20 minutes, or until golden brown.

Uncle's note: "Sometimes you have to hop and skip to find what connects you from one side to the other."
